Overview

The University of Regina offers a Masters of Science in Computer Science (Data Science) aimed at developing experts in data analysis, machine learning, and modern big data tools. This program combines academic rigor with hands-on practical experience to equip graduates for careers in data science across industries.

This program provides students with a solid foundation in data science concepts, including statistical modeling, machine learning, big data analytics, and data visualization. The curriculum is designed to balance theoretical knowledge with practical skills, giving students the opportunity to work on hands-on projects, collaborate with industry partners, and apply their learning to solve real-world problems.

Students will learn from experienced faculty who are actively engaged in cutting-edge research. The program's flexible structure allows students to tailor their learning experience by selecting electives that match their career goals, whether that involves data engineering, business analytics, or artificial intelligence. The University of Regina also offers a vibrant academic community with access to resources such as research labs, career services, and networking events, helping students to maximize their educational experience.

Course Rationale

Our program emphasizes practical learning through partnerships with industry, research opportunities, and a focus on ethical data practices. Students participate in real-world projects to understand how data science contributes to solving global challenges like climate change, healthcare, and more. Additionally, our emphasis on responsible data management and ethical AI ensures that graduates are well-prepared to make informed decisions that positively impact their communities.

Courses in This Program
  • First Semester (Fall)
    CS 701: Introduction to Data Science & Analytics (3 credits)
    CS 702: Database Management Systems for Data Science (3 credits)
  • Second Semester (Winter)
    CS 703: Statistical Methods for Data Science (3 credits)
    CS 704: Introduction to Machine Learning (3 credits)
  • Third Semester (Spring/Summer)
    CS 705: Deep Learning Foundations (3 credits)
    CS 706: Data Engineering & Big Data Technologies (3 credits)
  • Fourth Semester (Fall)
    CS 707: Data Visualization & Storytelling (3 credits)
    CS 708: Ethical AI & Data Science Practices (3 credits)
  • Fifth Semester (Winter)
    CS 718: Data Science Seminar (0 credits)
    CS 719: Data Science Project (6 credits)
